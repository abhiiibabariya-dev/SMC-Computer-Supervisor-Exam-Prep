## A. General Microbiology (Q1-Q25)

**Q1.** Father of Microbiology:
A) Robert Koch | B) **Antonie van Leeuwenhoek** | C) Louis Pasteur | D) Joseph Lister
**Answer: B) Antonie van Leeuwenhoek**

**Q2.** Father of Modern Microbiology / Germ Theory:
A) Leeuwenhoek | B) **Louis Pasteur** | C) Robert Koch | D) Fleming
**Answer: B) Louis Pasteur**

**Q3.** Koch's postulates are used to:
A) Classify bacteria | B) **Prove disease-causing organisms** | C) Stain bacteria | D) Culture bacteria
**Answer: B) Prove disease-causing organisms**

**Q4.** Gram staining differentiates bacteria based on:
A) Shape | B) Size | C) **Cell wall composition** | D) Motility
**Answer: C) Cell wall composition**

**Q5.** Gram positive bacteria have thick layer of:
A) Lipopolysaccharide | B) **Peptidoglycan** | C) Chitin | D) Cellulose
**Answer: B) Peptidoglycan**

**Q6.** Which is NOT a type of bacteria based on shape?
A) Cocci (spherical) | B) Bacilli (rod) | C) Spirilla (spiral) | D) **Amoeba (irregular)**
**Answer: D) Amoeba (irregular) — Amoeba is a protozoan**

**Q7.** Bacteria reproduce by:
A) Budding | B) **Binary fission** | C) Spore formation | D) Meiosis
**Answer: B) Binary fission**

**Q8.** Endospores are formed by:
A) E. coli | B) **Bacillus & Clostridium** | C) Staphylococcus | D) Streptococcus
**Answer: B) Bacillus & Clostridium**

**Q9.** Obligate aerobes require:
A) No oxygen | B) **Oxygen for growth** | C) CO2 only | D) Nitrogen only
**Answer: B) Oxygen for growth**

**Q10.** Obligate anaerobes are killed by:
A) Water | B) Heat | C) **Oxygen** | D) Light
**Answer: C) Oxygen**

**Q11.** Facultative anaerobes can grow:
A) Only with O2 | B) Only without O2 | C) **With or without O2** | D) Only in CO2
**Answer: C) With or without O2**

**Q12.** The study of fungi is called:
A) Virology | B) Bacteriology | C) **Mycology** | D) Parasitology
**Answer: C) Mycology**

**Q13.** The study of viruses is called:
A) Mycology | B) **Virology** | C) Bacteriology | D) Protozoology
**Answer: B) Virology**

**Q14.** Viruses are:
A) Prokaryotic | B) Eukaryotic | C) **Obligate intracellular parasites** | D) Free-living organisms
**Answer: C) Obligate intracellular parasites**

**Q15.** Bacterial cell wall is made of:
A) Cellulose | B) Chitin | C) **Peptidoglycan (Murein)** | D) Keratin
**Answer: C) Peptidoglycan (Murein)**

**Q16.** Flagella is responsible for:
A) Reproduction | B) **Motility** | C) Respiration | D) Protection
**Answer: B) Motility**

**Q17.** Plasmid is:
A) Cell membrane | B) **Extra-chromosomal circular DNA** | C) Ribosome | D) Flagellum
**Answer: B) Extra-chromosomal circular DNA**

**Q18.** Which organism causes food poisoning (toxin in food)?
A) Salmonella | B) **Staphylococcus aureus** | C) E. coli | D) Vibrio cholerae
**Answer: B) Staphylococcus aureus**

**Q19.** Salmonella causes:
A) Cholera | B) **Typhoid fever** | C) TB | D) Plague
**Answer: B) Typhoid fever**

**Q20.** Vibrio cholerae causes:
A) Typhoid | B) Dysentery | C) **Cholera** | D) Plague
**Answer: C) Cholera**

**Q21.** Mycobacterium tuberculosis causes:
A) Leprosy | B) **Tuberculosis** | C) Cholera | D) Tetanus
**Answer: B) Tuberculosis**

**Q22.** Clostridium tetani causes:
A) Botulism | B) Gangrene | C) **Tetanus (lockjaw)** | D) Cholera
**Answer: C) Tetanus (lockjaw)**

**Q23.** Penicillin was discovered by:
A) Koch | B) Pasteur | C) **Alexander Fleming** | D) Jenner
**Answer: C) Alexander Fleming (1928)**

**Q24.** Minimum temperature for bacterial growth is called:
A) Optimum | B) Maximum | C) **Minimum growth temperature** | D) Death point
**Answer: C) Minimum growth temperature**

**Q25.** Thermophilic bacteria grow best at:
A) 0-20°C | B) 20-40°C | C) **45-80°C** | D) Below 0°C
**Answer: C) 45-80°C**

## B. Water & Food Microbiology (Q26-Q45)

**Q26.** Indicator organism for water contamination:
A) Salmonella | B) **E. coli (Coliform)** | C) Staphylococcus | D) Bacillus
**Answer: B) E. coli (Coliform)**

**Q27.** MPN stands for:
A) Maximum Probable Number | B) **Most Probable Number** | C) Minimum Possible Number | D) Most Possible Number
**Answer: B) Most Probable Number**

**Q28.** MPN test is used to detect:
A) Viruses in water | B) **Coliform bacteria in water** | C) Heavy metals | D) pH
**Answer: B) Coliform bacteria in water**

**Q29.** Acceptable coliform count in drinking water:
A) 10/100 mL | B) 1/100 mL | C) **0/100 mL (zero)** | D) 100/100 mL
**Answer: C) 0/100 mL — Drinking water should be free of coliforms**

**Q30.** BOD stands for:
A) Basic Oxygen Demand | B) **Biochemical Oxygen Demand** | C) Biological Oxygen Density | D) Base Oxidation Demand
**Answer: B) Biochemical Oxygen Demand**

**Q31.** High BOD indicates:
A) Clean water | B) **Polluted water** | C) Alkaline water | D) Acidic water
**Answer: B) Polluted water (more organic matter = more O2 needed)**

**Q32.** COD stands for:
A) Carbon Oxygen Demand | B) **Chemical Oxygen Demand** | C) Coliform Oxygen Density | D) Combined Oxygen Deficiency
**Answer: B) Chemical Oxygen Demand**

**Q33.** Water purification by chlorination kills bacteria by:
A) Heating | B) Filtration | C) **Oxidation** | D) Freezing
**Answer: C) Oxidation**

**Q34.** Residual chlorine in treated water should be:
A) 0 mg/L | B) **0.2-0.5 mg/L** | C) 2-5 mg/L | D) 10 mg/L
**Answer: B) 0.2-0.5 mg/L**

**Q35.** Membrane filter technique gives results in:
A) 1-2 hours | B) **24 hours** | C) 72 hours | D) 1 week
**Answer: B) 24 hours**

**Q36.** Pasteurization of milk is done at:
A) 50°C for 10 min | B) **63°C for 30 min (or 72°C for 15 sec)** | C) 100°C for 1 min | D) 121°C for 15 min
**Answer: B) 63°C for 30 min (LTLT) or 72°C for 15 sec (HTST)**

**Q37.** HACCP stands for:
A) Health And Control Critical Points | B) **Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points** | C) Hospital And Clinical Control Plan | D) Health Assessment and Care Plan
**Answer: B) Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points**

**Q38.** Food spoilage by bacteria is indicated by:
A) Color change only | B) **Off-odor, slime, gas production** | C) Weight loss only | D) No visible change
**Answer: B) Off-odor, slime, gas production**

**Q39.** Clostridium botulinum causes:
A) Typhoid | B) **Botulism (food poisoning from canned food)** | C) Cholera | D) Dysentery
**Answer: B) Botulism**

**Q40.** FSSAI stands for:
A) Food Safety Standards Agency of India | B) **Food Safety and Standards Authority of India** | C) Federal Safety Standards Authority | D) Food Security and Safety Authority
**Answer: B) Food Safety and Standards Authority of India**

**Q41.** Standard Plate Count (SPC) measures:
A) pH | B) **Total viable bacteria count** | C) Coliform only | D) Virus count
**Answer: B) Total viable bacteria count**

**Q42.** Agar is obtained from:
A) Bacteria | B) Fungi | C) **Seaweed (Red algae)** | D) Animals
**Answer: C) Seaweed (Red algae)**

**Q43.** Turbidity in water indicates:
A) Purity | B) **Suspended particles / contamination** | C) High oxygen | D) Low minerals
**Answer: B) Suspended particles / contamination**

**Q44.** pH of safe drinking water:
A) 4.0-5.0 | B) **6.5-8.5** | C) 9.0-10.0 | D) 11.0-12.0
**Answer: B) 6.5-8.5 (as per IS 10500 / WHO)**

**Q45.** TDS in drinking water should be below:
A) 100 mg/L | B) **500 mg/L** | C) 1000 mg/L | D) 2000 mg/L
**Answer: B) 500 mg/L (desirable limit)**

## C. Immunology & Serology (Q46-Q60)

**Q46.** ELISA stands for:
A) Enzyme Limited Immune Sorbent Assay | B) **Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay** | C) Electronic Liquid Immune Standard Assay | D) Enzymatic Linked Immune Standard Assay
**Answer: B) Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay**

**Q47.** ELISA is commonly used to detect:
A) Blood group | B) **HIV antibodies** | C) Blood sugar | D) Cholesterol
**Answer: B) HIV antibodies**

**Q48.** Western Blot is a:
A) Screening test | B) **Confirmatory test for HIV** | C) Blood grouping test | D) Liver function test
**Answer: B) Confirmatory test for HIV**

**Q49.** Antibodies are produced by:
A) RBC | B) Neutrophils | C) **B-lymphocytes (Plasma cells)** | D) Platelets
**Answer: C) B-lymphocytes (Plasma cells)**

**Q50.** Which immunoglobulin is most abundant in blood?
A) IgA | B) IgM | C) **IgG** | D) IgE
**Answer: C) IgG**

**Q51.** IgE is associated with:
A) Immunity | B) **Allergic reactions** | C) Blood clotting | D) Digestion
**Answer: B) Allergic reactions**

**Q52.** First antibody produced in immune response:
A) IgG | B) **IgM** | C) IgA | D) IgD
**Answer: B) IgM**

**Q53.** Vaccine provides which type of immunity?
A) Natural passive | B) **Active artificial** | C) Innate | D) None
**Answer: B) Active artificial immunity**

**Q54.** Widal test detects antibodies against:
A) E. coli | B) **Salmonella typhi (Typhoid)** | C) Malaria | D) TB
**Answer: B) Salmonella typhi**

**Q55.** VDRL test is used for:
A) Hepatitis | B) HIV | C) **Syphilis** | D) Malaria
**Answer: C) Syphilis**

**Q56.** Mantoux test is used for:
A) Typhoid | B) HIV | C) **Tuberculosis (TB)** | D) Hepatitis
**Answer: C) Tuberculosis**

**Q57.** ASO (Anti-Streptolysin O) test detects:
A) Typhoid | B) **Streptococcal infection** | C) TB | D) Malaria
**Answer: B) Streptococcal infection**

**Q58.** CRP (C-Reactive Protein) is a marker for:
A) Diabetes | B) **Inflammation/Infection** | C) Anemia | D) Kidney disease
**Answer: B) Inflammation/Infection**

**Q59.** Autoimmune disease means:
A) Low immunity | B) **Body attacks its own cells** | C) No immunity | D) Allergy to food
**Answer: B) Body attacks its own cells**

**Q60.** Which cells are affected by HIV?
A) RBC | B) Platelets | C) **CD4+ T-lymphocytes** | D) Neutrophils
**Answer: C) CD4+ T-lymphocytes**

## D. Lab Techniques & Instruments (Q61-Q80)

**Q61.** Autoclave works on the principle of:
A) Dry heat | B) **Moist heat under pressure** | C) Filtration | D) Radiation
**Answer: B) Moist heat under pressure**

**Q62.** Autoclave standard conditions:
A) 100°C, 10 min | B) **121°C, 15 psi, 15 minutes** | C) 160°C, 1 hour | D) 80°C, 30 min
**Answer: B) 121°C, 15 psi, 15 minutes**

**Q63.** Hot air oven is used for:
A) Sterilizing liquids | B) **Sterilizing glassware (dry heat)** | C) Incubation | D) Centrifuging
**Answer: B) Sterilizing glassware (dry heat) — 160°C for 1 hour**

**Q64.** Pour plate method is used for:
A) Staining | B) **Counting colony forming units (CFU)** | C) Biochemical test | D) Serological test
**Answer: B) Counting colony forming units (CFU)**

**Q65.** Streak plate method is used for:
A) Counting bacteria | B) **Isolating pure cultures** | C) Staining | D) Biochemical test
**Answer: B) Isolating pure cultures**

**Q66.** MacConkey agar is:
A) Enrichment media | B) **Selective & differential media** | C) Transport media | D) Anaerobic media
**Answer: B) Selective & differential media (for Gram-negative)**

**Q67.** Blood agar is classified as:
A) Selective media | B) **Enriched media** | C) Transport media | D) Minimal media
**Answer: B) Enriched media**

**Q68.** Laminar air flow provides:
A) Vacuum | B) Pressure | C) **Sterile/HEPA filtered air** | D) Hot air
**Answer: C) Sterile/HEPA filtered air**

**Q69.** PCR stands for:
A) Protein Chain Reaction | B) **Polymerase Chain Reaction** | C) Peptide Cross Reaction | D) Primary Culture Reaction
**Answer: B) Polymerase Chain Reaction**

**Q70.** PCR is used to:
A) Count bacteria | B) **Amplify DNA** | C) Measure pH | D) Stain cells
**Answer: B) Amplify DNA**

**Q71.** Spectrophotometer works based on:
A) Heat absorption | B) **Beer-Lambert Law (light absorption)** | C) Centrifugal force | D) Pressure
**Answer: B) Beer-Lambert Law**

**Q72.** Colony forming unit (CFU) is expressed as:
A) mg/mL | B) **CFU/mL or CFU/g** | C) pH units | D) Percentage
**Answer: B) CFU/mL or CFU/g**

**Q73.** Incubation temperature for most pathogenic bacteria:
A) 25°C | B) **37°C** | C) 45°C | D) 56°C
**Answer: B) 37°C**

**Q74.** Gas pak jar is used for:
A) Aerobic culture | B) **Anaerobic culture** | C) Centrifuging | D) Staining
**Answer: B) Anaerobic culture**

**Q75.** Crystal violet is the primary stain in:
A) Ziehl-Neelsen stain | B) **Gram staining** | C) Acid-fast stain | D) Spore stain
**Answer: B) Gram staining**

**Q76.** Iodine in Gram staining acts as:
A) Decolorizer | B) Counterstain | C) **Mordant (fixes dye)** | D) Solvent
**Answer: C) Mordant**

**Q77.** Safranin in Gram staining is:
A) Primary stain | B) Mordant | C) Decolorizer | D) **Counterstain**
**Answer: D) Counterstain (stains Gram-negative pink)**

**Q78.** IMViC tests are used to identify:
A) Fungi | B) Virus | C) **Enterobacteriaceae (E. coli vs Klebsiella)** | D) TB
**Answer: C) Enterobacteriaceae**

**Q79.** IMViC pattern for E. coli is:
A) - - + + | B) **+ + - -** | C) + - + - | D) - + - +
**Answer: B) + + - - (Indole+, MR+, VP-, Citrate-)**

**Q80.** Catalase test differentiates:
A) Gram + from Gram - | B) **Staphylococcus from Streptococcus** | C) Aerobic from anaerobic | D) Virus from bacteria
**Answer: B) Staphylococcus (catalase +) from Streptococcus (catalase -)**
