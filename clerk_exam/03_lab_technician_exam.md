## A. Blood Chemistry & Tests (Q1-Q20)

**Q1.** Normal fasting blood glucose level is:
A) 40-70 mg/dL | B) **70-110 mg/dL** | C) 120-160 mg/dL | D) 160-200 mg/dL
**Answer: B) 70-110 mg/dL**

**Q2.** HbA1c test measures blood sugar control over:
A) 1 week | B) 1 month | C) **2-3 months** | D) 6 months
**Answer: C) 2-3 months**

**Q3.** Normal serum creatinine level in adults:
A) **0.7-1.3 mg/dL** | B) 2-4 mg/dL | C) 5-7 mg/dL | D) 8-10 mg/dL
**Answer: A) 0.7-1.3 mg/dL**

**Q4.** BUN stands for:
A) Basic Urine Nitrogen | B) **Blood Urea Nitrogen** | C) Blood Unit Nitrogen | D) Bile Urea Nitrogen
**Answer: B) Blood Urea Nitrogen**

**Q5.** Which enzyme is a marker for liver damage?
A) Amylase | B) Lipase | C) **SGPT (ALT)** | D) Creatine Kinase
**Answer: C) SGPT (ALT)**

**Q6.** Normal serum cholesterol level should be below:
A) 100 mg/dL | B) 150 mg/dL | C) **200 mg/dL** | D) 300 mg/dL
**Answer: C) 200 mg/dL**

**Q7.** Which lipoprotein is called "good cholesterol"?
A) LDL | B) VLDL | C) **HDL** | D) Chylomicron
**Answer: C) HDL**

**Q8.** Serum bilirubin is elevated in:
A) Anemia | B) **Jaundice** | C) Diabetes | D) Hypertension
**Answer: B) Jaundice**

**Q9.** Normal blood pH is:
A) 6.8-7.0 | B) 7.0-7.2 | C) **7.35-7.45** | D) 7.5-7.8
**Answer: C) 7.35-7.45**

**Q10.** SGOT (AST) enzyme is primarily found in:
A) Kidney | B) Lungs | C) **Heart & Liver** | D) Stomach
**Answer: C) Heart & Liver**

**Q11.** Troponin test is used to diagnose:
A) Diabetes | B) Liver disease | C) **Heart attack (MI)** | D) Kidney failure
**Answer: C) Heart attack (MI)**

**Q12.** Which test is used to measure kidney function?
A) LFT | B) **RFT (Renal Function Test)** | C) CBC | D) Lipid profile
**Answer: B) RFT (Renal Function Test)**

**Q13.** Normal serum sodium level:
A) 100-120 mEq/L | B) **136-145 mEq/L** | C) 150-170 mEq/L | D) 180-200 mEq/L
**Answer: B) 136-145 mEq/L**

**Q14.** Normal serum potassium level:
A) 1.5-2.5 mEq/L | B) **3.5-5.0 mEq/L** | C) 6-8 mEq/L | D) 9-11 mEq/L
**Answer: B) 3.5-5.0 mEq/L**

**Q15.** Amylase enzyme is used to diagnose:
A) Heart disease | B) **Pancreatitis** | C) Liver disease | D) Anemia
**Answer: B) Pancreatitis**

**Q16.** GTT stands for:
A) General Test for Thyroid | B) **Glucose Tolerance Test** | C) Globulin Total Test | D) Growth Tissue Test
**Answer: B) Glucose Tolerance Test**

**Q17.** Normal serum albumin level:
A) 1-2 g/dL | B) **3.5-5.0 g/dL** | C) 6-8 g/dL | D) 9-10 g/dL
**Answer: B) 3.5-5.0 g/dL**

**Q18.** Uric acid is elevated in:
A) Diabetes | B) **Gout** | C) Anemia | D) TB
**Answer: B) Gout**

**Q19.** Normal blood urea level:
A) 5-10 mg/dL | B) **15-40 mg/dL** | C) 50-80 mg/dL | D) 100-150 mg/dL
**Answer: B) 15-40 mg/dL**

**Q20.** Which test confirms diabetes mellitus?
A) CBC | B) ESR | C) **Fasting Blood Sugar + HbA1c** | D) LFT
**Answer: C) Fasting Blood Sugar + HbA1c**

## B. Haematology (Q21-Q40)

**Q21.** Normal haemoglobin level in adult males:
A) 8-10 g/dL | B) 10-12 g/dL | C) **13-17 g/dL** | D) 18-22 g/dL
**Answer: C) 13-17 g/dL**

**Q22.** Normal WBC count:
A) 1000-2000/cu mm | B) **4000-11000/cu mm** | C) 15000-20000/cu mm | D) 25000-30000/cu mm
**Answer: B) 4000-11000/cu mm**

**Q23.** Normal platelet count:
A) 50,000-1,00,000/cu mm | B) **1,50,000-4,00,000/cu mm** | C) 5,00,000-8,00,000/cu mm | D) 10,00,000/cu mm
**Answer: B) 1,50,000-4,00,000/cu mm**

**Q24.** ESR stands for:
A) Estimated Serum Rate | B) **Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate** | C) Enzyme Substrate Reaction | D) Electrolyte Serum Ratio
**Answer: B) Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate**

**Q25.** Which blood cell fights infection?
A) RBC | B) **WBC** | C) Platelet | D) Plasma
**Answer: B) WBC**

**Q26.** Blood group that is universal donor:
A) A | B) B | C) AB | D) **O negative**
**Answer: D) O negative**

**Q27.** Blood group that is universal recipient:
A) O | B) A | C) B | D) **AB positive**
**Answer: D) AB positive**

**Q28.** Rh factor was discovered from:
A) Rat | B) **Rhesus monkey** | C) Rabbit | D) Rhinoceros
**Answer: B) Rhesus monkey**

**Q29.** PT and APTT tests are used for:
A) Liver function | B) Kidney function | C) **Blood clotting disorders** | D) Diabetes
**Answer: C) Blood clotting disorders**

**Q30.** Reticulocyte count indicates:
A) Infection | B) **Bone marrow activity** | C) Liver function | D) Kidney function
**Answer: B) Bone marrow activity**

**Q31.** Leishman stain is used for:
A) Bacteria | B) **Blood smear (peripheral)** | C) Tissue section | D) Urine
**Answer: B) Blood smear (peripheral)**

**Q32.** Normal ESR value in males (Westergren method):
A) **0-15 mm/hr** | B) 20-30 mm/hr | C) 40-50 mm/hr | D) 60-80 mm/hr
**Answer: A) 0-15 mm/hr**

**Q33.** Sickle cell anemia is caused by defect in:
A) WBC | B) Platelets | C) **Haemoglobin (HbS)** | D) Plasma
**Answer: C) Haemoglobin (HbS)**

**Q34.** Thalassemia is most common in:
A) North India | B) South India | C) **Gujarat & Maharashtra** | D) Northeast India
**Answer: C) Gujarat & Maharashtra**

**Q35.** MCV, MCH, MCHC are part of:
A) LFT | B) RFT | C) **Red cell indices (CBC)** | D) Lipid profile
**Answer: C) Red cell indices (CBC)**

**Q36.** Normal bleeding time (Duke's method):
A) **1-5 minutes** | B) 8-12 minutes | C) 15-20 minutes | D) 25-30 minutes
**Answer: A) 1-5 minutes**

**Q37.** Normal clotting time:
A) 1-2 minutes | B) **3-8 minutes** | C) 12-15 minutes | D) 20-25 minutes
**Answer: B) 3-8 minutes**

**Q38.** Hemophilia is a disorder of:
A) RBC | B) WBC | C) **Clotting factors** | D) Platelets
**Answer: C) Clotting factors**

**Q39.** DLC stands for:
A) Direct Lipid Count | B) **Differential Leukocyte Count** | C) Diagnostic Lab Certificate | D) Disease Level Category
**Answer: B) Differential Leukocyte Count**

**Q40.** Which WBC type is most abundant?
A) Lymphocyte | B) Monocyte | C) **Neutrophil** | D) Eosinophil
**Answer: C) Neutrophil**

## C. Microbiology (Q41-Q60)

**Q41.** Gram staining was developed by:
A) Robert Koch | B) Louis Pasteur | C) **Hans Christian Gram** | D) Alexander Fleming
**Answer: C) Hans Christian Gram**

**Q42.** Gram positive bacteria appear:
A) Red/Pink | B) **Purple/Violet** | C) Green | D) Yellow
**Answer: B) Purple/Violet**

**Q43.** Gram negative bacteria appear:
A) Purple | B) **Red/Pink** | C) Blue | D) Green
**Answer: B) Red/Pink**

**Q44.** Which organism causes Tuberculosis?
A) Streptococcus | B) **Mycobacterium tuberculosis** | C) E. coli | D) Staphylococcus
**Answer: B) Mycobacterium tuberculosis**

**Q45.** Ziehl-Neelsen staining is used for:
A) Fungi | B) Virus | C) **Acid-fast bacteria (TB)** | D) Parasites
**Answer: C) Acid-fast bacteria (TB)**

**Q46.** ELISA test is used for:
A) Blood grouping | B) **HIV detection** | C) Malaria | D) Pregnancy
**Answer: B) HIV detection**

**Q47.** Widal test is used to diagnose:
A) Malaria | B) **Typhoid fever** | C) Dengue | D) TB
**Answer: B) Typhoid fever**

**Q48.** Autoclave sterilization temperature:
A) 100°C | B) **121°C at 15 psi for 15 min** | C) 160°C | D) 200°C
**Answer: B) 121°C at 15 psi for 15 min**

**Q49.** Culture media for TB:
A) Blood agar | B) MacConkey agar | C) **Lowenstein-Jensen (LJ) medium** | D) Chocolate agar
**Answer: C) Lowenstein-Jensen (LJ) medium**

**Q50.** Penicillin was discovered by:
A) Robert Koch | B) Louis Pasteur | C) **Alexander Fleming** | D) Edward Jenner
**Answer: C) Alexander Fleming**

**Q51.** E. coli is found naturally in:
A) Skin | B) **Human intestine** | C) Lungs | D) Blood
**Answer: B) Human intestine**

**Q52.** Blood agar is used to identify:
A) Acid-fast bacteria | B) **Haemolytic bacteria** | C) Fungi | D) Virus
**Answer: B) Haemolytic bacteria**

**Q53.** MacConkey agar is used for:
A) TB | B) Fungi | C) **Gram negative enteric bacteria** | D) Virus
**Answer: C) Gram negative enteric bacteria**

**Q54.** Which test confirms HIV?
A) ELISA | B) Widal | C) **Western Blot** | D) Mantoux
**Answer: C) Western Blot**

**Q55.** Malaria parasite is detected by:
A) Widal test | B) **Peripheral blood smear (thick & thin)** | C) ELISA | D) Culture
**Answer: B) Peripheral blood smear (thick & thin)**

**Q56.** Dengue is transmitted by:
A) Anopheles mosquito | B) **Aedes aegypti mosquito** | C) Housefly | D) Ticks
**Answer: B) Aedes aegypti mosquito**

**Q57.** Normal body temperature:
A) 35°C | B) 36°C | C) **37°C (98.6°F)** | D) 38°C
**Answer: C) 37°C (98.6°F)**

**Q58.** Hot air oven sterilization temperature:
A) 100°C for 1 hour | B) 121°C for 15 min | C) **160°C for 1 hour** | D) 200°C for 30 min
**Answer: C) 160°C for 1 hour**

**Q59.** Which hepatitis virus is transmitted through blood?
A) Hepatitis A | B) **Hepatitis B & C** | C) Hepatitis E | D) All
**Answer: B) Hepatitis B & C**

**Q60.** Father of Microbiology:
A) Robert Koch | B) **Antonie van Leeuwenhoek** | C) Louis Pasteur | D) Joseph Lister
**Answer: B) Antonie van Leeuwenhoek**

## D. Lab Equipment & Safety (Q61-Q80)

**Q61.** Centrifuge is used to:
A) Heat samples | B) **Separate blood components** | C) Sterilize | D) Count cells
**Answer: B) Separate blood components**

**Q62.** Spectrophotometer measures:
A) Temperature | B) Weight | C) **Light absorption** | D) Pressure
**Answer: C) Light absorption**

**Q63.** Haemocytometer is used to count:
A) Bacteria | B) **Blood cells** | C) Protein | D) Glucose
**Answer: B) Blood cells**

**Q64.** EDTA anticoagulant is used in which tube?
A) Red top | B) Blue top | C) **Purple/Lavender top** | D) Green top
**Answer: C) Purple/Lavender top**

**Q65.** Sodium citrate tube (blue top) is used for:
A) CBC | B) **Coagulation tests (PT, APTT)** | C) Blood sugar | D) Culture
**Answer: B) Coagulation tests (PT, APTT)**

**Q66.** pH meter measures:
A) Temperature | B) **Acidity/Alkalinity** | C) Weight | D) Density
**Answer: B) Acidity/Alkalinity**

**Q67.** Biohazard symbol color is:
A) Red | B) Green | C) **Orange/Red** | D) Blue
**Answer: C) Orange/Red**

**Q68.** Needle stick injury protocol includes:
A) Ignore it | B) **Wash, report, test for HIV/HBV/HCV** | C) Apply alcohol only | D) Resume work
**Answer: B) Wash, report, test for HIV/HBV/HCV**

**Q69.** PPE stands for:
A) Personal Physical Equipment | B) **Personal Protective Equipment** | C) Professional Protocol Equipment | D) Patient Protection Equipment
**Answer: B) Personal Protective Equipment**

**Q70.** Cold chain temperature for vaccines:
A) -20°C | B) 0°C | C) **2-8°C** | D) 15-20°C
**Answer: C) 2-8°C**

**Q71.** Incubator standard temperature for bacterial culture:
A) 25°C | B) **37°C** | C) 45°C | D) 56°C
**Answer: B) 37°C**

**Q72.** Which sample tube is used for blood glucose?
A) Purple top | B) Blue top | C) **Grey top (sodium fluoride)** | D) Red top
**Answer: C) Grey top (sodium fluoride)**

**Q73.** Micropipette is used to measure:
A) Large volumes | B) **Very small liquid volumes (microliters)** | C) Temperature | D) Weight
**Answer: B) Very small liquid volumes (microliters)**

**Q74.** Laminar air flow cabinet is used for:
A) Centrifuging | B) **Aseptic/sterile work** | C) Washing | D) Autoclaving
**Answer: B) Aseptic/sterile work**

**Q75.** Quality control in lab ensures:
A) Cost reduction | B) **Accuracy and reliability of results** | C) Speed of testing | D) Staff management
**Answer: B) Accuracy and reliability of results**

**Q76.** Normal urine pH:
A) 2-3 | B) **4.6-8.0** | C) 9-10 | D) 11-12
**Answer: B) 4.6-8.0**

**Q77.** Glucometer is used to measure:
A) Haemoglobin | B) **Blood glucose** | C) Cholesterol | D) Creatinine
**Answer: B) Blood glucose**

**Q78.** Benedict's test is used for:
A) Protein in urine | B) **Glucose/reducing sugars in urine** | C) Blood in urine | D) Bacteria in urine
**Answer: B) Glucose/reducing sugars in urine**

**Q79.** Microscope magnification = Eyepiece x Objective. If eyepiece is 10x and objective is 40x, total magnification:
A) 50x | B) 100x | C) **400x** | D) 4000x
**Answer: C) 400x**

**Q80.** Waste segregation - red bag is for:
A) General waste | B) **Contaminated/infectious waste** | C) Sharps | D) Chemicals
**Answer: B) Contaminated/infectious waste**
